# Physio, Recovery, and Workout Planning Harness

## 1. Purpose

This harness is built to help you manage three things at the same time without them fighting each other:

* **Workout planning**
* **Physio / rehab work**
* **Recovery management**

The goal is simple: train hard enough to improve, recover well enough to absorb it, and manage niggles before they become injuries.

---

## 2. Core Rules

### Rule 1 — Rehab is not optional

Physio work is not an extra. If you are carrying pain, tightness, instability, or a known issue, rehab is part of training load.

### Rule 2 — Recovery drives progression

You do not progress from the session itself. You progress from adapting to it. Poor sleep, high soreness, high stress, and unresolved pain should directly affect the next session.

### Rule 3 — Separate pain from fatigue

* **Fatigue** = expected tiredness, heaviness, general soreness
* **Pain** = local, sharp, worsening, compensatory, or movement-limiting sensation

Fatigue can often be trained through.
Pain should change the plan.

### Rule 4 — One hard goal at a time

Do not stack:

* hard workout progression
* aggressive rehab loading
* poor sleep
* calorie deficit
* extra life stress

If one goes up, another needs to come down.

---

## 3. Daily Readiness Check

Use this every morning before deciding the day.

Score each from **1 to 5**:

* **Sleep quality**
* **Energy**
* **Muscle soreness**
* **Joint / tendon pain**
* **Mental stress**
* **Motivation to train**

### Traffic light logic

#### Green day

* Sleep acceptable
* Pain low
* Fatigue manageable
* Motivation decent

**Action:** follow plan as written.

#### Yellow day

* Moderate fatigue
* Poor sleep
* Mild pain or stiffness
* Motivation low

**Action:** reduce volume 20–30%, reduce intensity, or swap hard work for controlled aerobic / technique / mobility.

#### Red day

* Sharp pain
* Limping / compensation
* Pain worsening during warm-up
* Exhaustion
* Very poor sleep for multiple days

**Action:** no hard session. Replace with recovery, rehab, mobility, easy aerobic work, or full rest.

## 4. Session Decision Tree

Before every session ask:

### A. What is today’s main goal?

Choose one:

* Build fitness
* Build strength
* Build skill / technique
* Restore movement
* Reduce pain / calm tissue
* Recover

If you cannot answer this clearly, the session is too vague.

### B. What is the current limiter?

Choose the main one:

* Cardio fatigue
* Muscle fatigue
* Joint / tendon pain
* Stiffness / mobility restriction
* Poor sleep / high stress
* Time availability

### C. What should win today?

Priority order:

1. Protect tissue
2. Maintain consistency
3. Progress training
4. Add extras

## 5. The Weekly Structure

This is the base framework.

## Weekly Buckets

Every week should include:

* **2–4 key workout sessions**
* **2–6 physio / rehab blocks**
* **1–3 mobility sessions**
* **1–2 deliberate recovery sessions**
* **At least 1 lighter day**

### Session categories

#### 1. Key Workout

Main performance builder.
Examples:

* interval run
* threshold bike
* long endurance session
* strength workout

#### 2. Support Workout

Useful but not critical.
Examples:

* easy zone 2
* technique swim
* light gym
* drills

#### 3. Physio / Rehab

Targeted tissue loading or control work.
Examples:

* calf raises for Achilles
* adductor rehab
* glute med control
* isometrics for pain relief
* foot / ankle stability

#### 4. Recovery Session

Designed to improve freshness, not fitness.
Examples:

* easy spin
* walk
* mobility flow
* breathing work
* soft tissue routine

## 8. Rehab / Physio Harness

This is the part most people mess up: they either do random exercises, or they do the right exercises with no progression logic.

For each issue, track these fields:

## Problem profile

* Area:
* Suspected issue:
* Trigger movements:
* Pain scale at rest:
* Pain scale during activity:
* Morning stiffness:
* What improves it:
* What aggravates it:

## Rehab goal

Examples:

* reduce reactive pain
* restore range of motion
* improve load tolerance
* improve tendon capacity
* improve single-leg stability
* return to running without flare-up

## Rehab method type

Choose one primary mode:

* **Isometric** for pain modulation / early loading
* **Slow heavy strength** for tissue capacity
* **Tempo control** for movement quality
* **Mobility** for ROM restriction
* **Plyometric / return-to-sport** for later-stage tolerance

## Progression ladder

1. Pain calming
2. Basic loading
3. Strength capacity
4. Dynamic control
5. Sport-specific return

### Basic rules

* Pain during rehab should usually stay in a manageable range
* Symptoms should settle back to baseline by the next day
* If next-day pain clearly spikes, the load was too much

## 9. Recovery Harness

Recovery should be planned with the same seriousness as sessions.

## Recovery pillars

### Sleep

Track:

* total hours
* sleep quality
* wake-ups
* consistency of bedtime

### Nutrition

Track:

* did you underfuel?
* protein intake covered?
* hydration adequate?
* carbs around key sessions?

### Nervous system / stress

Track:

* mental load
* irritability
* inability to relax
* elevated resting fatigue feeling

### Tissue state

Track:

* stiffness on waking
* joint irritation
* tendon reactivity
* DOMS severity

---

## 10. Recovery Interventions Menu

Choose based on need, not randomly.

### If general fatigue is high

* reduce intensity
* easy aerobic flush
* earlier bedtime
* more carbs
* remove non-essential volume

### If tendon / joint irritation is high

* reduce impact
* use isometrics
* shorten range if needed
* swap intensity for controlled loading
* avoid stacking hard days

### If muscle soreness is high

* easy movement
* mobility
* hydration
* protein and carbs
* avoid max strength or high eccentric load

### If mental stress is high

* shorten session
* choose simpler session
* avoid maximal intensity
* walking, breathing, light movement

## 11. Workout Harness

Each workout should be categorized before it is added.

For every session define:

* **Type:** endurance / intervals / strength / mobility / rehab / recovery
* **Goal:** what adaptation you want
* **Cost:** low / medium / high
* **Risk:** low / medium / high for current injury status
* **Recovery demand:** low / medium / high

### Example session classification

| Session              | Goal                |        Cost | Injury Risk | Recovery Demand |
| -------------------- | ------------------- | ----------: | ----------: | --------------: |
| Easy zone 2 bike     | aerobic maintenance |         low |         low |             low |
| Threshold run        | speed endurance     |        high | medium-high |            high |
| Lower-body strength  | strength            | medium-high |      medium |     medium-high |
| Achilles calf raises | tendon capacity     |  low-medium |         low |      low-medium |
| Mobility flow        | restore movement    |         low |         low |             low |

This stops you from stacking too many expensive sessions together.

## 12. Load Management Rules

Use these rules when combining training and rehab.

### Do not stack on the same day unless deliberate

Avoid combining all of these on one day:

* hard run
* heavy lower-body strength
* aggressive tendon rehab
* poor sleep

### Respect tissue-specific stress

Examples:

* Achilles: running + plyos + calf strength all hit the same system
* Patellar tendon: jumps + squats + hard descents stack load fast
* Hamstring: sprinting + deadlifts + long stride running stack risk

### Use minimum effective dose

When tired or sore, ask:

* what is the smallest session that still keeps momentum?

Sometimes that is:

* 30 min easy aerobic
* 15 min rehab
* 10 min mobility

That still counts.

---

## 14. Red Flags

Stop pretending it is just tightness if you have:

* sharp pain that changes mechanics
* swelling
* night pain
* loss of strength
* progressive worsening over days
* numbness / tingling
* repeated failed return to training

That is the point to reduce load and get proper assessment.

## 18. What to Track Minimum

If you only track a few things, track these:

* session type
* session RPE
* pain before / during / after
* sleep quality
* morning stiffness
* overall fatigue

That is enough to make better decisions than most people do.
